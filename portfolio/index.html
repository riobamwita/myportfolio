LiDAR Analysis

LiDAR-based Elevation Structure

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) provides high-resolution 3D information about terrain and built/vegetation structures. In this example of Logan International Airport, LiDAR captures detailed elevation and structural data of buildings, and surrounding topographic features. Such data can complement satellite imagery by accurately mapping structures and land cover, helping to detect subtle changes in urban development and infrastructure.

Change Detection

Using satellite imagery from 2017 and 2025, we can observe forest cover dynamics over time. The side-by-side comparison highlights areas of deforestation and regeneration.

2017&2025

Change Detection

Change detection is performed by comparing pixel values or classified land cover classes from the two images. Statistics can then be generated to quantify the extent of forest loss, gain, or regeneration. This methodology can be applied to a wide range of applications, including urban expansion monitoring, agricultural land change, and environmental risk assessment.

In client projects, I have used similar techniques to provide actionable spatial analyses, maps, and statistics for decision-making in land management, environmental studies, and resource monitoring.

Spatial Modelling in GIS

Spatial modelling in GIS is a powerful tool for analyzing geographic data and identifying patterns across both environmental and urban contexts. Here are two examples demonstrating its application:

Air Pollution Exposure

This map illustrates air pollution exposure across Central Nakuru County. The Central Nakuru CBD is highlighted in purple and red, indicating high pollutant concentrations due to dense urban activities and traffic emissions. In contrast, rural areas to the east in Nyandarua County are shown in yellow and green, reflecting lower pollution levels and cleaner air. Spatial modelling allows integration of emission sources, land use, population distribution, and meteorological data to assess exposure, guide public health interventions, and support planning decisions.

Spatial Distribution Analysis

This map presents the spatial distribution of service centres within the Nakuru West region. The analysis highlights the geographic arrangement and density of facilities across the area, allowing identification of clustered zones and underserved locations. By examining spatial patterns, planners can assess service accessibility, evaluate equity in facility allocation, and support infrastructure development strategies. Such distribution modelling is essential in regional planning, public service optimization, and socio-economic development assessments.

Urban Heat Island

The second example demonstrates urban heat intensity in the Kisumu City area, particularly around residential zones and the CBD. The heat island map shows "intense" red areas where built-up infrastructure and dense human activity raise surface temperatures. Surrounding rural and vegetated areas appear green, indicating lower temperature intensity. This type of spatial modelling is valuable for urban planning, environmental management, and identifying zones vulnerable to climate-related risks.

3D Modelling
