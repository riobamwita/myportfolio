Air Pollution Exposure Mapping

Mapping and analysis of population exposure to air pollutants by integrating emission sources, land use patterns, meteorological data, and settlement distribution.

The map shows that the central part of Nakuru County, particularly around the town center, exhibits high to very high exposure levels (red to purple), with PM2.5 concentrations exceeding approximately 36–48 µg/m³ and NO₂ levels above 15–20 µmol/m². This high exposure is mainly due to dense urban development, heavy traffic, and industrial activities in the town. Moving eastwards toward Nyandarua County, the exposure gradually decreases to moderate-low and low levels (yellow to green), with PM2.5 around 0–24 µg/m³ and NO₂ around 0–10 µmol/m², reflecting areas that are less densely populated and less developed. In the future, more detailed maps incorporating population distribution could provide better insights into the areas and people most affected by air pollution.

Urban Heat Island Analysis

Assessment of temperature variations within urban environments using remote sensing and spatial analysis to identify heat stress zones and support climate-sensitive urban planning.

The Land Surface Temperature (LST) map for the Winam Gulf region, focusing on Kisumu City, shows the spatial distribution of surface heat across urban and rural areas for 2023–2024. On the map, the urban areas of Kisumu City, shown in red, are intensely hotter compared to surrounding rural zones in green, clearly highlighting the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect. The mean temperature in urban areas was 41.9°C, while rural areas averaged 34.7°C, resulting in a UHI intensity of 7.2°C. This demonstrates that built-up zones in Kisumu City trap significantly more heat, which can impact local climate, health, and energy demand. Detailed graphical maps showing temperature variation and vegetation coverage are also available for deeper insights and planning purposes.
