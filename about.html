About Me

I am a Geographer and alumnus of Moi University. My academic and professional training is grounded in GIS, Remote Sensing, land surveying, cartography, and environmental assessment, with a strong focus on practical, field-based and data-driven applications.

I have hands-on experience in seasonal land survey work and government-linked geospatial assignments, where I have participated in cadastral, geodetic, plain, and topographic surveys. My field experience includes operating RTK GNSS, GPS, and Total Station equipment for accurate data collection, boundary verification, and site mapping.

In the area of GIS and Remote Sensing, I have worked on spatial data processing, environmental monitoring, land use analysis, hazard and risk mapping, and site suitability analysis. I am proficient in using ArcGIS, QGIS, R Studio, Flow-3D Hydro, AutoCAD, Google Earth Pro, and Google Earth Engine to produce maps, spatial datasets, and analytical outputs for research, planning, and decision-making.

My professional exposure includes an industrial attachment at the Directorate of Resource Surveys and Remote Sensing (DRSRS) in Nairobi, where I participated in geospatial workshops, assisted in government mapping tasks, and supported infrastructure and road network mapping under technical supervision.

Beyond fieldwork and analysis, I have applied my skills in developing digital solutions, including a functional web-based Survey Data Portal designed to support the storage, organization, and retrieval of survey maps, coordinates, and project files. I also take on freelance GIS projects involving environmental mapping, land fragmentation analysis, and land use change detection.

Overall, my work is driven by a strong interest in applying geospatial technologies to solve real-world problems in land management, environmental planning, and sustainable development.
